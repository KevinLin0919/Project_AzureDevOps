# Finance News - March 25, 2026

### 1. Global Markets Rally on U.S.-Iran Peace Plan
Stock markets worldwide surged following reports that the U.S. has proposed a 15-point peace plan to end the three-week-old war with Iran.
* **U.S. Markets:** Stock futures climbed as investors reacted to the potential for a diplomatic resolution.
* **Indian Markets:** The BSE Sensex soared over 1,200 points (1.63%) to close at 75,273.45, while the Nifty 50 rose nearly 400 points to settle above 23,250.
* **Asian & European Markets:** Japan’s Nikkei 225 jumped 2.9%, and major European indices like the FTSE 100 and DAX saw gains of 1% to 1.6% in early trading.

### 2. Energy Prices Plunge
Oil prices fell by more than 5% as hopes for a ceasefire eased concerns over supply disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz.
* **Brent Crude:** Dropped to approximately $98–$99 per barrel, falling below the $100 mark for the first time in weeks.
* **WTI Crude:** Declined to around $87 per barrel.

### 3. Banking & Regulatory Updates
* **U.S. Basel Reforms:** U.S. banking regulators proposed maintaining current capital treatments for trade finance products, a move welcomed by the industry. The plan would cut capital requirements for the largest U.S. lenders (like JPMorgan and Citi) by about 4.8%.
* **HDFC Bank:** Shares of India’s largest private lender rose over 3% to an intraday high of ₹791.35.
* **Compliance AI:** CUBE and Microsoft announced a partnership to integrate regulatory intelligence into Azure, aiming to automate compliance for global financial institutions.

### 4. Corporate M&A and Earnings
* **Moniepoint Acquisition:** Nigerian fintech giant Moniepoint acquired Orda Africa to expand its "Moniebook" platform into the hospitality and restaurant sector.
* **ADP Results:** ADP announced it will release its third-quarter fiscal 2026 financial results on April 29, 2026.
* **IPL Sale:** A consortium including Blackstone and the Aditya Birla Group reportedly acquired the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) for $1.8 billion.

### 5. Economic Outlook
* **S&P Global:** Warned that while a short-lived conflict might only cause temporary spikes, a prolonged war poses significant stagflation risks.
* **UK Trade:** The UK announced a £13 million "Aid for Trade" package at the WTO conference in Cameroon.
